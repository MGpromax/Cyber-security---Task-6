Passwords — Examples for Evaluation (Do Not Reuse)

NOTE: These are demonstration examples only. Do not use them in real accounts. Do not paste real passwords into online checkers.

Legend:
- naive_entropy_bits = quick upper‑bound estimate assuming full randomness
- effective strength may be lower if the string includes dictionary words/predictable structure

1) password
   - length: 8 | charset: lowercase only
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 8 × log2(26) ≈ 37.6
   - notes: dictionary word → extremely weak; trivial in dictionary attacks

2) Password1
   - length: 9 | charset: upper+lower+digits
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 9 × log2(62) ≈ 53.6
   - notes: common pattern (Capitalized word + digit). Lower effective strength.

3) P@ssw0rd!
   - length: 9 | charset: upper+lower+digits+symbols
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 9 × log2(95) ≈ 59.1
   - notes: leetspeak of a common word; pattern-aware attackers catch this

4) Sunshine2024
   - length: 12 | charset: upper+lower+digits
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 12 × log2(62) ≈ 71.5
   - notes: common word + year; predictable; weaker than entropy suggests

5) G00d#Luck!!
   - length: 11 | charset: upper+lower+digits+symbols
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 11 × log2(95) ≈ 72.3
   - notes: dictionary words + repeats; medium in checkers; avoid patterns

6) Tr0ub4dor&3
   - length: 11 | charset: upper+lower+digits+symbols
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 11 × log2(95) ≈ 72.3
   - notes: famous example; structure reduces real strength to medium

7) correct horse battery staple
   - passphrase: 4 random words (space‑separated)
   - passphrase_entropy ≈ 4 × log2(2048) ≈ 44 bits (assuming 2048‑word list)
   - notes: memorable; stronger with 5–6 words and separators

8) mango-salsa-hedgehog-laser-planet
   - passphrase: 5 random words with dashes
   - passphrase_entropy ≈ 5 × log2(2048) ≈ 55 bits (separators help a bit)
   - notes: good balance of length and memorability

9) 2F!oH9v$Tq#1wRmZ
   - length: 16 | charset: upper+lower+digits+symbols
   - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 16 × log2(95) ≈ 105.1
   - notes: very strong if generated randomly by a manager

10) A!b2C@d3E#f4G$
    - length: 14 | charset: upper+lower+digits+symbols
    - naive_entropy_bits ≈ 14 × log2(95) ≈ 92.0
    - notes: appears patterned (alternating). True strength depends on randomness.

Tip: For real accounts, prefer either 16–20 character random strings (via a password manager) or 5–7 random‑word passphrases with separators, and always enable MFA.
